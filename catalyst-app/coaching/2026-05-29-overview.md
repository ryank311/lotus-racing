## 7. Drills

### Drill 1 — T12 Oak Tree reference point
**Problem:** Turning in 1–2 car-lengths too early, leaving 0.7 m/s exit speed on the table.  
**Exercise:** Pick a new turn-in marker that is 1 car-length later than current. Aim to have the car still rotating at the late apex cone — you should feel like you are using more road on exit than seems comfortable. Confirm with exit speed > 21.5 m/s. Do this as a focus lap (not pushing the rest of the lap) — commit to the late apex on every T12 entry for a full session.

### Drill 2 — Climbing Esses commitment lap
**Problem:** T7 apex is 1–2 m/s below best case in all but 2 laps across the entire weekend.  
**Exercise:** On the uphill approach, identify your current lift/adjustment point before T7. Move it 50 m later (or remove it entirely if already near-flat). You have run 52 m/s through there — the speed is achievable. The goal is to normalize 51–52 m/s as the expected speed, not the exceptional speed. Do 3 consecutive laps targeting this single corner, note the apex speed on the Catalyst readout after each lap.

### Drill 3 — T4b throttle pick-up
**Problem:** T4b exit speed inconsistent (26–28 m/s), occasionally sub-26.  
**Exercise:** At T4b, once the car is rotated and pointing toward T5a, apply throttle **earlier** — the camber and downhill gradient will catch any oversteer. Target: throttle on before the apex. Look for T5a entry speed above 30.5 m/s (currently 29–31 m/s, PB 31.2). Use the Catalyst segments to monitor S4 — any S4 time below 27.8 s means the Snake worked.

### Drill 4 — Afternoon tire pressure check
**Problem:** The May 24 afternoon sessions (15:34–18:21) were 2–4 s slower than the morning despite dry conditions and no apparent incidents. Max longitudinal acceleration dropped from +6.4 to +3.4 m/s². This is a large reduction.  
**Exercise (off-track):** At the next event, take hot pressures immediately after a flying lap in the afternoon sessions. Target 32 F / 34 R (Bridgestone RE-71RZ confirmed targets). If pressures are above 36 psi, bleed 2 psi and do another lap. Overinflated RE-71Rs go greasy on center contact; the drop in long_accel suggests power-limited wheelspin or deliberate throttle reduction — both consistent with reduced rear grip.
