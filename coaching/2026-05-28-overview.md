### Daily pattern
- **Morning (13.9 °C)**: cool air, high straight-line top speed, but lower mid-corner grip in fast cambered corners. Wins S1, S2, S4, S8, S10.
- **Mid-day (17-19 °C)**: pace drops. The 983fe6a8 session pattern (uniform back-straight slowdown across all 5 laps) is the signature of traffic, not setup — 5 laps in a row at S6 ~32.0 vs PB 30.70.
- **Late afternoon (21.1 °C)**: tires hot-cycled, peak straight-line speed slightly down, but **the high-speed corners T7/T8/T9/T14 are all faster than morning**. Wins S3, S5, S6, S9.

### Tire compound observation (RE71-RZ specific)
The RE71-RZ "comes in" with heat. The morning lap had the front-straight peak (cool air, low pressure), but the Esses-corridor PBs came from the afternoon — the tire window for this compound, on this car, with these pressures, appears to be biased *warmer* than the morning conditions provided. Two practical implications:
1. **Sandbagging the morning sessions** by under-driving warm-up laps is leaving lap time on the table — the tires aren't getting to the window.
2. **For event days starting cold, plan to push harder earlier** in each session to heat-soak the tires faster. The morning PB was lap 5 of 9; the heat-soaked corner gains showed up only by the afternoon's lap 3.

### Setup status note
All 5 sessions were post-April rake/damper changes (120F/125R, dampers -12/-10/-10 F, -8/-6/-8 R). The Climbing Esses mistake-rate is still **5 of 32 laps (16%)** — improved from the 3-in-9 (33%) observation in the previous brief, but still the dominant source of variance. The setup changes helped; technique work is now the bottleneck.

## 6. Prioritised recommendations

### #1 — Late-brake Oak Tree (T12). **Expected gain: ~0.25-0.30 s in S7**
- **Where:** T12 apex 4133 m, braking initiation ~4060-4090 m.
- **What:** 3169a432 L6 demonstrated **T13 entry 29.1 m/s vs the PB-lap's 25.4** (a +5.1 m/s / +11 mph braking-initiation differential). Same T13 apex (21.3 vs 21.5), almost identical exit. This was not luck — that lap also has the S7 PB at 9.55.
- **How:** the back straight ends with 100+ mph speeds (~57.5 m/s on PB lap at 3900 m). Currently brakes go on around 3950 m. Try shifting brake application 30-50 m later — to ~4000 m. The Catalyst trace shows current peak brake decel happens between 3950-4050 m, then 5+ seconds of corner-speed scrubbing. Compressing the braking window keeps the back straight longer.
- **Reasoning:** S7 is the largest single leak in the PB lap (-0.31s). The capability is demonstrated. This is the highest-leverage corner of the lap.

### #2 — T4 NASCAR / T5 Snake speed carry. **Expected gain: ~0.30-0.40 s in S2+S3**
- **Where:** T4 apex 835 m, T5 apex 1045 m.
- **What:** unchanged from the previous brief — 0d9decc9 L8 demonstrated T4 apex 28.7 / T5 apex 21.6 vs PB-lap's 26.9 / 20.1. The L8 spin later in the lap didn't invalidate the demonstrated carry.
- **How:** later turn-in at T4, more trail-brake to rotate the car, exit T4 already on throttle and let the Snake inherit the speed.
- **Reasoning:** still the second-largest combined unlock (S2 +0.23, S3 +0.20).

### #3 — Heat-soak the tires earlier / link the afternoon's Esses-corridor corner speed to the morning's straight-line speed. **Expected gain: ~0.30-0.40 s combined across S4/S5/S6/S9**
- **Where:** T8, T9, T14 — the high-speed cambered corners.
- **What:** the afternoon session (49286453) carried T8 apex 39.1, T9 apex 20.2, T14 apex 32.0 — vs morning PB-lap's 37.7, 19.1, 31.1. The tire compound (RE71-RZ) needs heat that the cool-morning lap never achieved.
- **How:** two-pronged.
  1. **Session strategy**: push harder on warm-up laps (the morning's S1 PB came on lap 5, by which time the tires were finally in window). Don't treat lap 1 as a sighting lap — treat it as an explicit heat-soak.
  2. **Driver technique**: the corner-speed gain in the Esses corridor is partly platform (warmer tire grip), but the afternoon laps also show different *entry-speed strategy* into T9 (34.9 vs 31.1) — i.e. the driver was braver coming over the T8 crest and into T9. After more heat in the tires, commit to higher T9 entry.
- **Reasoning:** these corners feed each other: T8 sets up T9, T9 sets up the South Bend / Oak Tree Entry sequence. A faster apex in any one of them is worth ~0.1s, but compound effect across the corridor is realistic.
