## 6. Prioritised recommendations

### #1 — Late-brake T12 Oak Tree (S7 gain). **Expected: ~0.25-0.30 s**
- **Evidence:** 3169a432 L6 hit T13 entry 29.1 m/s vs PB-lap 25.4. Identical apex (21.3 vs 21.5), identical exit. The brake initiation point on `3169a432 L6` is ~40-50 m later than the PB lap.
- **Action:** push the braking marker from ~3950 m to ~4000 m. The Catalyst trace at 3900 m peaks at 57.5 m/s on the PB lap — that's already at terminal speed. The full braking phase 3950-4100 m can be compressed by ~40 m without changing the corner outcome.
- **Risk:** the highest-risk action of the three priorities (locking up into Oak Tree blocks the corner exit). Reduce brake bias 1 click rearward if traction is the limit.

### #2 — Carry T4-T5 momentum (S2+S3 gain). **Expected: ~0.35-0.45 s**
- **Evidence:** 0d9decc9 L8 demonstrated T4 apex 28.7 + T5 apex 21.6 cleanly before its later spin. Combined S2+S3 PB gap = 0.43 s.
- **Action:** later turn-in at T4, more trail-brake, accept that the exit will sit further right (closer to track-out) — the existing line is over-slowing the corner. The Exige's stiff front (550 lb/in) punishes early turn-in.
- **Risk:** low. T4-T5 is a low-consequence error zone — Snake exit is wide.

### #3 — Build T8/T6 commitment in the Esses corridor (S4 gain). **Expected: ~0.20-0.30 s**
- **Evidence:** ebe23e23 L6 ran S4 at 27.47 (PB lap 27.66) with T6 apex 26.0 / T8 apex 39.0. PB lap is at 37.7 on T8 — the slowest T8 of any clean lap in the dataset. **There is no laptime payoff to driving T8 below 38 m/s; the corner is essentially flat in a properly-loaded Exige with the diffuser working.**
- **Action:** treat T8 as a flat-throttle corner, not a lifted one. The fact that the PB lap lifts here is a holdover from pre-rake/pre-damper days. The car has changed; the inputs haven't.
- **Risk:** this is a high-speed blind crest, so commitment errors here are high-consequence. Validate with a single deliberate flat-T8 attempt on a clear lap with hot tires before committing it to a normal hot lap.

## 7. Drills

1. **Oak Tree late-brake reference (priority #1):** in your second session of next track day (after the tires are warm), pick one lap to deliberately overshoot the current brake marker by ~30 m. Don't try to make a complete lap — just probe the brake-zone. Validate with Catalyst: T13 entry ≥27 m/s on the next clean lap. Goal is the partial gain (from 25.4 to ~27), not the full 29.1 from `3169a432 L6` on day one.

2. **T4 carry drill (priority #2):** three deliberate laps where the only goal is matching `0d9decc9 L8` S2 (7.94 s). Brake earlier, lighter, with later turn-in. Don't worry about the rest of the lap. Target T4 apex ≥28 m/s confirmed in Catalyst.

3. **T8 commitment drill (priority #3):** after the rake/damper changes the diffuser is now producing rear downforce in T8. The PB lap doesn't trust it yet. Run one deliberate flat-throttle T8 (no lift across the crest at 2380-2425 m) on a lap with hot tires and clear track. Goal: T8 apex ≥38.5 m/s — the corner has been done at 39.1 multiple times. Validate by trace; if the car is light on the crest after the rake change, *that's the data point telling you the rear ride height needs to come down further* (next setup iteration, not next lap).
