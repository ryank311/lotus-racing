# 2021 Corvette Z51
I own a C8 Chevrolette Corvette that I take for track-day weekends to VIR (Virginia International Raceway). This car is also a street/touring car that I use and it is mostly stock. I have a trailer that I use to tow it when I go to track events.

A typical laptime for me is 2:16 on the VIR full course, and I have PB's in the 2:15s. I have a garmin catalyst that I use to collect video and race data.

I intend to use Chevrolet's track alignment to get the most out of the car when I take it for track days

I have the 2LT with the Z51 package with the hydraulic front lift and Magna-Ride the suspension.

## Driver Notes
- Primary handling concern: **understeer**, especially at Turn 1 (Horseshoe) and Oak Tree (Turns 11-12)
- Some **oversteer at Hog Pen** (Turns 16-17)
- Some **oversteer braking off the back straight into the roller coaster** (Turn 13 area) -- high-speed braking zone where the road drops away
- **Not fully confident at high speed through the upper Climbing Esses** (Turns 7-9)
- Dad is the primary driver for this car
- Top speed ~150 mph off the back straight, ~140 mph off the front straight (slightly faster on best laps)

## Setup Research: Reducing Understeer & Optimizing for VIR

### 1. Alignment: Get the Full GM Track Spec (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

Current alignment is partway to track spec but short in all key areas:

| Parameter | Current | GM Track Spec | Gap |
|-----------|---------|---------------|-----|
| Front Camber | -2.4 deg | **-3.0 deg** | 0.6 deg short |
| Rear Camber | -1.9 deg | **-2.5 deg** | 0.6 deg short |
| Front Caster | 7.5 deg | **+8.0 deg** | 0.5 deg short |
| Front Toe | 0.10 deg | 0.10 deg | At spec (consider 0.00 for sharper turn-in) |
| Rear Toe | 0.10 deg | 0.10 deg | At spec |

Achieving full track spec requires UCA washer relocation on all four corners (~$500-600, 2-3 hours). This is the single highest-impact change for reducing understeer at Turn 1 and Oak Tree.

### 2. Tire Strategy Note

The Pirelli Trofeo R / Goodyear SC3R mixed-brand combo is workable but not ideal. Different thermal falloff curves mean the front-to-rear grip balance shifts throughout each session. For a long-term setup, matching all four corners with SC3R (recommended) or Trofeo R would be more predictable. The wider front (265 vs 245) is a clear improvement for understeer regardless of compound.

### 3. Brake Setup: Adequate for Current Pace

Current XP12/XP10 + GiroDisc + SRF is well-validated for intermediate HPDE. Key action items: verify all Z51 brake cooling ducts are installed (dealers were inconsistent), and install rear lower control arm ducts before each track event. See Brake-Guide.md for details.

### 4. Critical Technique Finding: eDiff and Trail Braking

The C8's electronic differential locks more aggressively under braking, which can cause understeer. Some fast C8 drivers have found that braking hard/straight/late and then fully releasing the brake BEFORE turn-in (rather than trail braking) reduces push. This is worth experimenting with at Turn 1 and comparing via Garmin Catalyst data. See VIR-Full-Course-Guide.md for corner-by-corner technique.

### 5. Lap Time Context

Randy Pobst ran 2:00.96 in a completely stock C8 Z51 on stock tires at VIR. At 2:16, the car is not the limiting factor -- there is ~15 seconds of driver improvement available. Near-term target: 2:12-2:13 with proper alignment, tire pressures, and trail braking improvements.

### 6. Recommended Changes Priority

1. **GET FULL GM TRACK ALIGNMENT** -- -3.0F / -2.5R camber, +8.0 caster, consider 0.00 front toe (~$500-600)
2. **Set tire pressures for understeer** -- 26F/28R cold, or 25F/28R if understeer is severe
3. **Install wider front tires** -- 265/30/19 on Apex VS5RS wheels
4. **Verify brake cooling ducts** -- install rear lower control arm ducts for track
5. **Run in Track mode** with PTM on
6. **Experiment with trail braking vs. brake-then-turn** technique at Turn 1 and Oak Tree
7. **Raise rear ride height** via Z51 spring perches (~2 turns / ~1/4 inch) if understeer persists
8. **Aftermarket sway bars** (aFe Control 3-way adjustable) -- stiffen rear to reduce understeer
