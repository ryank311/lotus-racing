## Chevrolet Track Alignment Specs

GM publishes a "track alignment" for the C8 Z51 that goes beyond the street specs. The current alignment is partway there but not at full track specs.

### Street vs. Track Alignment Comparison

| Parameter | Street Spec | Track Spec | Current | Gap to Track |
|-----------|-------------|------------|---------|-------------|
| **Front Camber** | -0.7 deg | **-3.0 deg** | -2.4 deg | **0.6 deg short** |
| **Rear Camber** | -1.3 deg | **-2.5 deg** | -1.9 deg | **0.6 deg short** |
| **Front Caster** | +7.3 deg | **+8.0 deg** | 7.5 deg | **0.5 deg short** |
| Front Toe | 0.10 deg total | 0.10 deg total | 0.10 deg total | At spec |
| Rear Toe | 0.10 deg total | 0.10 deg total | 0.10 deg total | At spec |

### How to Achieve Full Track Alignment

Getting from your current alignment to full track specs requires **relocating the upper control arm (UCA) washers** on all four corners. This is a 2-3 hour job at an alignment shop and costs approximately $500-600 total. The UCA washers are what provide the additional camber and caster range beyond the standard adjustment.

**This is the single highest-impact, lowest-cost change available for reducing understeer.**

### Community Consensus

From CorvetteForum and MidEngineCorvetteForum, experienced C8 track drivers report:

- Most dual-use drivers run a **compromise** around -2.0 front / -1.5 rear for street-heavy use
- **Dedicated track drivers run the full -3.0 / -2.5** and report it is fine for street use with acceptable tire wear if toe is set correctly
- The **camber split** (front more negative than rear) is more important than the absolute numbers for controlling understeer. Your current split is -0.5 deg (front more negative). The track spec split is also -0.5 deg but at higher absolute values
- **Zero front toe** is popular among track drivers for improved turn-in. It is liveable on the street. Going from 0.10 deg to 0.00 deg would improve turn-in response, which directly helps with the understeer at Turn 1 and Oak Tree
- **Caster at +8.0** adds straight-line stability and dynamic camber gain in corners -- meaning the front tires gain effective camber as you add steering lock, giving more grip exactly when you need it

### Recommendation

**Get the full GM track alignment before the next track day.** The gains from going -2.4/-1.9 to -3.0/-2.5 with +8.0 caster are significant and directly address the understeer complaint. The additional front camber and caster will noticeably improve front grip and turn-in at Turn 1 and Oak Tree.

If you want a single intermediate step: prioritize the front camber and caster first (-3.0 front camber, +8.0 caster). These have the biggest effect on turn-in and trail braking grip.

## Tire Strategy

### Current Plan: Pirelli Trofeo R Front / Goodyear Supercar 3R Rear

**Assessment: Workable but not recommended as a long-term strategy.**

The fundamental issue with mixing brands is that the two compounds have different thermal behaviors:
- **Trofeo R:** Reaches peak grip quickly (laps 2-3) but falls off after 1-2 fast laps. Better in high-speed corner entry.
- **Supercar 3R:** Takes longer to come up to temperature (laps 4-6) but maintains consistent grip across the entire session. Excels in braking zones.

This means the front-to-rear grip balance will **shift throughout every session**. Early in a session, the fronts will be grippiest (helpful for understeer). Later as the fronts fade and rears stay strong, the car may become unexpectedly loose.

In Grassroots Motorsports' head-to-head testing on a McLaren 720S, the Trofeo R matched the SC3R's best lap (1:18.2) but couldn't sustain it.

**For HPDE use at 8/10ths**, the combo is acceptable. The SC3R on the rear provides the stability you want. But it introduces an unpredictable variable that makes setup tuning harder.

### Recommended Tire Setup (If Buying Fresh)

**Best option for dedicated track tires:** Goodyear SC3R all four corners in matched sizes.

The SC3R is the consensus pick among fast Corvette track drivers. Key advantages:
- Very high peak grip with excellent consistency
- Minimal wear after 10+ heat cycles
- Proven on C8s at VIR specifically

**Recommended sizes for the Apex VS5RS 19x9.5 / 19x11.5 wheels:**
- Front: 265/35R19 SC3R (the /35 aspect ratio keeps overall diameter closer to stock)
- Rear: 325/30R19 SC3R

**Alternative:** Keep the Cup 2s for street driving and wet-weather track days. They are actually quite good at the intermediate HPDE level.

### What About the 265/30R19 Trofeo R?

This size has questionable availability -- it does not appear on most standard Trofeo R size charts. The closest confirmed 19-inch sizes are 245/35R19, 295/30R19, 305/30R19, and 325/30R19. **Verify this size can actually be purchased before committing.**

If you go Trofeo R, the 265/35R19 is a more commonly available size (note the /35 vs /30 aspect ratio difference -- this changes overall diameter).

### Wheel Setup Analysis: 19/19 vs. Stock 19/20

The move from stock 19/20 staggered to 19/19 staggered (Apex VS5RS) is a solid upgrade:

**Pros:**
- Weight savings on the rear (19" wheel lighter than 20")
- Wider front tire (265 vs 245) -- adds ~20mm of contact patch, directly reduces understeer
- More tire options available when both wheels are the same diameter

**Cons:**
- Overall rear diameter changes: stock 305/30R20 is ~27.2" diameter. A 325/30R19 is ~25.3" -- about 1.9" shorter. This lowers the rear slightly and affects speedometer accuracy
- The 325 on 11.5" wheel is at the narrow end of the recommended range (11-12" rim width) -- the tire may "balloon" slightly

**Apex's fitment note:** They recommend -2.5 front / -2.0 rear camber minimum for their wider wheel fitments on the C8. Your current -2.4/-1.9 is slightly under this, reinforcing the need for the track alignment.

### Tire Pressure Targets for VIR

| | Front | Rear |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold start (70-80F ambient)** | 26-27 psi | 28-29 psi |
| **Cold start (85F+ ambient)** | 24-25 psi | 26-27 psi |
| **Hot target** | 32-34 psi | 31-33 psi |

**GM's official track recommendation:** 23 psi cold, targeting 32-35 psi hot.

**John Heinricy (GM performance engineer) tested at VIR and recommended:** 25.5 psi front / 29 psi rear cold, targeting 33-34 psi front / 31 psi hot rear.

**To reduce understeer via pressure:** Lower front pressures by 1-2 psi OR raise rear pressures by 1-2 psi. This increases the front contact patch relative to the rear.

**Process:**
1. Set cold pressures before Session 1 based on ambient temperature
2. Run the warm-up session without adjusting
3. After Session 2, check hot pressures immediately off track. Bleed down if over target
4. Use a chalk/paint stick across the tread face to check wear patterns -- this tells you more about pressure optimization than numbers alone

**For R-compound tires (SC3R, Trofeo R):**
- SC3R tends to like the higher end of hot range (33-35 psi)
- Trofeo R can get "greasy" above 34 psi hot -- does better at 32-33 psi

### Tire Temperature Management

| Compound | Optimal Surface Temp | Too Cold | Too Hot |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goodyear SC3R | 160-200F | Below 140F | Above 220F |
| Pirelli Trofeo R | 130-180F | Below 120F | Above 200F |
| Michelin Cup 2 | 140-190F | Below 130F | Above 210F |

**VIR's abrasive surface** generates more tire heat than average, which means:
- Tires that overheat easily (Yokohama A052, Bridgestone RE71RS) are poor choices
- The SC3R's heat tolerance is an excellent match for VIR
- The Trofeo R's falloff tendency is more pronounced on abrasive surfaces

**Practical tips:**
- Always do a cool-down lap before pitting in
- Park in shade between sessions if possible
- Front tires generally don't overheat at VIR -- getting them up to temp is the bigger concern
- Rear tires are the temperature-critical pair -- monitor closely

## Handling Balance: Reducing Understeer

### Why the C8 Understeers (By Design)

Chevy engineers intentionally tuned the C8 to understeer. Tadge Juechter (Executive Chief Engineer) said his "biggest fear was oversteer when designing the C8" because in a mid-engine car, when oversteer occurs, it happens faster and is much harder to catch. "It took Porsche decades to deal with the issue and they had to get it right the first time."

The result: the C8 pushes wide under the limit rather than snapping loose. This is safer but costs lap time, especially at Turn 1 and Oak Tree where trail braking is critical.

### Setup Adjustments to Reduce Understeer (Priority Order)

| Priority | Adjustment | Cost | Impact | Notes |
|----------|-----------|------|--------|-------|
| **1** | **Full GM track alignment** (-3.0F/-2.5R camber, +8.0 caster) | ~$500-600 | **Highest** | Single biggest improvement. Requires UCA washer relocation |
| **2** | **Tire pressures** -- lower front 1-2 psi relative to rear | Free | **High** | Quick trackside adjustment |
| **3** | **Wider front tires** (265 vs 245) | Already purchased | **High** | 20mm more contact patch on the front |
| **4** | **Zero front toe** (reduce from 0.10 to 0.00) | Part of alignment | **Medium** | Improves turn-in crispness |
| **5** | **Raise rear ride height** via Z51 spring perches (~2 turns / ~1/4 inch) | Free (DIY) | **Medium** | Shifts weight balance forward |
| **6** | **Aftermarket sway bars** -- stiffen rear, soften front | ~$700-1200 | **Medium-High** | aFe Control 3-way adjustable bars are the top recommendation. The C8 does NOT have adjustable stock sway bars |

### Sway Bar Notes

The stock C8 sway bars are not adjustable. The aftermarket options:

- **aFe Control 3-way adjustable** -- the most recommended for C8 track use. Run front soft, rear stiff to reduce understeer
- **Eibach adjustable bars** -- also well-regarded, multiple settings per bar
- **BMR adjustable bars** -- another option in the same price range

For reducing understeer: a **stiffer rear sway bar** reduces rear grip in corners, allowing the car to rotate more. This is particularly effective at Turn 1 and Oak Tree where mid-corner understeer is the complaint. Start on the softest rear setting and work stiffer.

### Ride Height Considerations

**Front height:** The hydraulic front lift system **replaces the adjustable front spring seats**, so front height is NOT adjustable via the standard method. Aftermarket lowering kits from Lingenfelter (0.50-0.75" drop) and Paragon (0.75") work with the front lift and MagneRide, but these are beyond bolt-on territory.

**Rear height:** IS adjustable via the Z51 spring perches. Approximately 0.75 inch of total range available. Raising the rear by ~2 turns (~1/4 inch) shifts weight balance slightly forward, which helps with understeer.

**For understeer:** Raising the rear relative to the front is beneficial and can be done with just the rear spring perches at no cost.

## MagneRide Optimization

### Drive Modes on Track

- **Track Mode** sets MagneRide dampers to maximum factory stiffness and enables PTM (Performance Traction Management, exclusive to MagneRide-equipped cars)
- **Z-Mode** allows custom configuration mixing suspension, steering, and traction settings. You can select Track suspension with Sport traction, for example
- **For VIR:** Use Track mode. For advanced drivers, PTM Mode 5 ("Race with Active Handling off") provides the most freedom

### Aftermarket MagneRide Tuning: DSC Sport Controller

The **DSC Sport V2/V3 controller** (~$2,000-2,500) is the primary aftermarket upgrade for MagneRide:

- Plug-and-play replacement for the factory Vehicle Dynamics Module (VDM)
- Dramatically expands the damper adjustment range and tunability
- Owners report it "transforms the car's handling"
- Retains full street comfort and front lift system functionality
- Competitive with passive coilovers in a similar price range while being much better on the street

**This is beyond bolt-on territory for now** but worth knowing about as a future upgrade path. At the intermediate HPDE level, the factory MagneRide in Track mode is adequate.

## Recommended Changes: Priority and Tracking

### Before Next Track Day

1. **GET THE FULL GM TRACK ALIGNMENT** -- -3.0 front / -2.5 rear camber, +8.0 caster. Consider zero front toe. This is the single most impactful change (~$500-600)
2. **Set tire pressures for understeer reduction** -- Start 26F/28R cold (or 25F/28R if understeer is severe)
3. **Install the wider front tires** (265/30/19 on Apex VS5RS wheels)
4. **Verify all Z51 brake cooling ducts are installed** (see Brake-Guide.md)
5. **Run in Track mode** with PTM on

### Future Considerations (In Order)

6. Raise rear ride height via Z51 spring perches (~1/4 inch)
7. Aftermarket sway bars (aFe Control 3-way adjustable) -- stiffen rear to reduce understeer
8. DSC Sport V2/V3 MagneRide controller for more suspension tuning range

### What to Evaluate After Each Change

Run 6-8 laps at consistent pace, then assess:
- [ ] Turn 1 understeer: better / same / worse?
- [ ] Oak Tree understeer: better / same / worse?
- [ ] Turn 13 oversteer (braking): better / same / worse?
- [ ] Hog Pen oversteer: better / same / worse?
- [ ] Climbing Esses confidence: higher / same / lower?
- [ ] Overall front-end grip: better / same / worse?
